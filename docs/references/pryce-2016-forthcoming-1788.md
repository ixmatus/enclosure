Where Kearfott's overview ([kearfott-2013-p1788-overview](kearfott-2013-p1788-overview.md))
records the working group's goals mid flight, Pryce's chapter explains the
finished design: flavors, the decoration system, Level 1 versus Level 2, and
why the standard is structured as it is. For clause by clause questions the
standard itself governs ([ieee-1788-2015](ieee-1788-2015.md)); for "why is it
this way" this chapter is the best open source.

The license picture deserves the registry's skepticism: Crossref metadata
records CC BY-NC 2.5 for the version of record, which is unusual for an LNCS
chapter, while the Springer landing page behaves as paywalled (redirects
through an authorization endpoint). The working group's own mailing list copy
on grouper.ieee.org is the accessible mirror; grouper.ieee.org has been
decommissioning for years, so that copy is rot risky and the 2018-11-25
Wayback snapshot above is the hedge (a fresh save was rate limited on
2026-06-11).
