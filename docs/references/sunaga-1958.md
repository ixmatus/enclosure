Sunaga worked out interval enclosure, directed rounding of endpoints, and even
the interval Newton idea in 1958. The standardization line this workspace
implements (Moore, Kulisch, 1788) descends through rediscovery rather than
citation, which is itself the registry's best argument for archiving: the
field lost this paper for decades because it lived in an out of print memoir
series. The reprint's permission note, quoted by Project Euclid, confirms the
original bibliographic data. Fresh Wayback saves were rate limited on
2026-06-11; the recorded snapshot is pre existing.
