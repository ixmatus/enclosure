Moore's 1966 monograph made interval arithmetic a discipline; Sunaga
([sunaga-1958](sunaga-1958.md)) got there first and was forgotten, and the
1788 standardization ([ieee-1788-2015](ieee-1788-2015.md)) is the line's
eventual codification. No legitimate online copy exists, so the durable
reference is bibliographic: publisher, year, LCCN. A paper copy is the
permacomputing vendoring move for a load bearing book; the status flips to
paper-copy-owned when the estate actually holds one, and until then this
entry is the visible gap.
