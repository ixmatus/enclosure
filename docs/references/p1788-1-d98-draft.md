What this draft grounds, with clause numbers as of D9.8:

- Clause 1.5, the meaning of conformance: decorations (5.2), required
  operations (6.7) at the required and recommended accuracies (6.5), string
  I/O (6.8.2, 6.8.3), and an interchange representation (7.3).
- Table 4.1, the required forward elementary battery of the SIMPLIFIED
  profile, which already includes the full trigonometric and hyperbolic sets
  with their inverses, `atan2`, `pown`/`pow`, `exp`/`exp2`/`exp10`,
  `log`/`log2`/`log10`, the integer functions (`sign`, `ceil`, `floor`,
  `trunc`, `roundTiesToEven`, `roundTiesToAway`), and `abs`/`min`/`max`.
- Clauses 4.5.3 through 4.5.7: cancellative subtraction, set operations, the
  `numsToInterval`/`textToInterval` constructors, the numeric functions
  (including `mid`/`rad` with "no value" on empty or unbounded input), and
  the boolean comparisons (`equal`, `subset`, `interior`, `disjoint` only).
- Annex A (informative), the load-bearing delta list: operations REQUIRED in
  the set-based flavor of full IEEE Std 1788-2015 but not required in
  1788.1: all reverse-mode elementary functions (10.5.4); two-output
  division `mulRevToPair` (10.5.5); the boolean orderings `less`,
  `precedes`, `strictLess`, `strictPrecedes` (10.5.10); the reduction
  operations `sum`, `dot`, `sumSquare`, `sumAbs` (12.2.12); and the exact
  text representation `intervalToExact`/`exactToInterval` (13.4). This annex
  is the source that settled the interval-1788 v1.0 ledger's
  required-versus-recommended boundary (bead enc-k8l).

Map versus territory, a note on the source rather than a correction of our
transcription: the Annex A line "Reduction operations (12.2.12)" is almost
certainly this draft's own misprint for 12.12.12. The final IEEE Std 1788-2015
groups its Level 2 operations under clause 12.12 (corroborated by the
arXiv:2308.10693 verbatim quotation of the final clause 12.12), and inside that
group the two output division sits at 12.12.3 per the post ballot mailing list
record ([p1788-mailing-list-threads](p1788-mailing-list-threads.md)). Our
consumers transcribe the draft's "12.2.12" faithfully where they cite this
draft; the disagreement with the final numbering is the draft's, and it is
recorded here so a reader does not read the draft label as the final clause.
